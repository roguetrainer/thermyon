# Portfolio E — Grand Challenges

**Primary audience:** Physicists, chemists, biologists, number theorists

---

## ASA for Physicists, Chemists & Number Theorists

Portfolio E applies the ASA's geometric framework to the grand challenge problems of physics, chemistry, and mathematics. These papers are more speculative than Portfolios A–D — they are working papers that identify structural correspondences and propose conjectures, rather than engineering implementations. The common thread is that exceptional Lie algebras and octonion geometry appear, unexpectedly but precisely, in the data of nuclear physics, electron shell structure, and the Riemann zeta function.

---

### For Physicists & Nuclear Chemists

**Nuclear Magic Numbers** (Paper 245) asks why atomic nuclei with 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126 protons or neutrons are anomalously stable. The standard explanation (the nuclear shell model with spin-orbit coupling) correctly predicts the numbers but offers no geometric reason for the sequence. Paper 245 observes that the magic numbers match the dimensions of representations of the exceptional Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}_2$ and its associated Jordan algebra $J^3(\mathbb{O})$. The conjecture is that the strong nuclear force geometry is non-associative at short range, and the magic numbers are the fingerprint of octonion symmetry in the strong interaction.

**Electron Shell Structure** (Paper 246) makes the analogous argument for the periodic table. The shell capacities 2, 8, 18, 32 are $2n^2$ for $n = 1, 2, 3, 4$ — the standard quantum-mechanical result. But Paper 246 shows that the *anomalies* in the filling order (the lanthanide contraction, the anomalous configurations of Cr, Cu, Mo, Pd, Ag, Au, and the f-block compression) are precisely accounted for by a $G_2$ rigidity constraint: the f-orbitals are geometrically frozen by the same rank-4 friction structure that appears in the Fano-Fisher metric. The lanthanide contraction is, on this view, a macroscopic consequence of the $G_2$ Casimir.

**The Fano-SYK Model** (Paper 267, also Portfolio A) connects the ASA to quantum gravity and holography via the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (SYK) model — a toy model of a black hole that is exactly solvable and saturates the chaos bound. The standard SYK model uses random all-to-all couplings over fermions; the Fano-SYK model replaces random couplings with the structured non-associative couplings of the octonion algebra. The Bruhat-Tits building of $G_2$ provides a natural $p$-adic bulk geometry, and the boundary theory is the Fano-structured CFT. This gives a concrete non-associative example of AdS/CFT and connects the ASA's computational framework to black hole information theory.

### For Number Theorists

**The ζ(21) Apéry Generalization** (Paper 265) asks whether Apéry's 1978 proof of the irrationality of $\zeta(3)$ extends to higher odd zeta values. Apéry's proof relies on a remarkable 3-term recurrence with integer coefficients. Paper 265 conjectures a 4-term recurrence for $\zeta(21)$, motivated by the observation that the cubic Pisot unit governing Apéry's recurrence is related to the $G_2$ Weyl group — and $\zeta(21)$ is the first odd zeta value whose index matches the dimension of the $G_2$ exceptional algebra.

**Geometric Shadows in Apéry's Polynomial** (Paper 266) analyses Apéry's polynomial $A_n(x) = \sum_k \binom{n}{k}^2 \binom{n+k}{k}^2 x^k$ through the lens of $G_2$ factorisation. The polynomial has a remarkable factorisation structure at Fano-prime values of $x$, and its trilinear form matches the index structure of the octonion associator. The paper conjectures that the "geometric shadow" of $\zeta(3)$'s irrationality is a Fano volume form on a $G_2$-symmetric polyhedron.

**Structural Observations on $J^3(\mathbb{O})$** (Paper 240) is the most speculative paper in Portfolio E: it identifies structural parallels between the automorphic forms of the exceptional Jordan algebra $J^3(\mathbb{O})$ and the distribution of Riemann zeta zeros, and poses a set of precise questions for automorphic forms specialists. It does not claim a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis, but identifies the Bruhat-Tits cospan of $G_2$ as a candidate geometric object in which a proof might live.

### For Biologists & Biochemists

*(See also Portfolio C, Paper 252 — Quantum Fano-Biology, forthcoming)*

The same non-associative geometry that governs nuclear magic numbers and electron shell anomalies also appears in the trace metal active sites of biological nanomachines. Zinc fingers, copper oxidases, and iron-sulfur clusters all involve ternary metal–ligand interactions whose coupling geometry matches the Fano 3-form. The conjecture (Paper 252, forthcoming) is that the *entatic state* — the strained, high-energy configuration of metal centres in enzymes — is a manifestation of geometric frustration in the $G_2$ vacuum: the enzyme holds its metal centre in a non-Fano configuration, storing potential energy that is released on substrate binding.
